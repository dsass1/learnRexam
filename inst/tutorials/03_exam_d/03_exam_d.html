Application 3 (6 points)

Superstores claim that more than 20% of shoppers are Members.

Use the sales_sample data and customer_type variable to conduct a hypothesis test for the proportion of superstore shoppers that are a Member of the superstore at the \(\alpha\) = 0.10 significance level. Are more than 30% of superstore shoppers Members?

\[H_0: \pi_{member} = 0.2\] \[H_A: \pi_{member} \ne 0.2\]


State your p-value and interpret your decision in the context of the problem.

sales_sample Data

This is a random sample taken from our population: shoppers at supercenter stores.

About the data: Customers were randomly chosen to review a product they bought at a superstore (Target, Walmart, Cloud9).

You are welcome to open the dataset in Posit Cloud as well if the information provided below is not sufficient for you.

Variables:

  • store – Identifies the store the shopper purchased from (Walmart, Target, Cloud9)
  • customer_type – “Are you a store member?” (Member, Normal)
  • gender – “Which gender do you identify as?” (Male, Female)
  • product_line – “What department is the products from?” (Electronics, Fashion, Food, Health, Lifestyle, Sports)”
  • payment – “Payment type (Cash, Credit card)”
  • `rating`` – “Rate your product and shopping experience on a scale of 1 to 10.”
  • price – “Total price paid for the product.”
  • time – “Time spent in the store in minutes.”
