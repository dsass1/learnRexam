The following applications use the survey dataset which has been preloaded for you.

Application 1 (3 points)

Suppose the number of minutes that a student athlete exercises each week follows a normal distribution with mean 500 and sd 100.

What proportion of athletes exercise between 350 and 650 minutes?

The top 20% of athletes exercise more than how many minutes?

Application 2 (6 points)

Suppose we are interested in examining the difference in proportion of students who take STAT 202 at NU that are Extrovert who go to “5+” parties a month and Introvert who go to “5+” parties a month.

In other words, use the personality and party_bin variables in the survey data to construct a 90% confidence interval for the difference in proportion of Extrovert s who attend 5+ parties and Introvert s who attend 5+ parties.


Interpret the confidence interval in the context of the problem. Then use the confidence interval to answer if there is a difference in proportions, if so, do extroverts or introverts attend more parties?

Application 3 (6 points)

Is there significant evidence that students who take STAT 202 at NU on average plan to marry before they turn 30?

Use the survey data to conduct a hypothesis test for the average age a student expects to marry at the \(\alpha\) = 0.02 significance level.

\[H_0: \mu_{marry} = 30\] \[H_A: \mu_{marry} \ne 30\]


State your p-value and interpret your decision in the context of the problem.
